# Status of the adc_cpp repository

Synthesis of six cross-checked audits (Architecture, Numerics/Science, Correctness/Robustness, Performance, Distributed/HPC, Tests/Docs). Each work item below has been cross-referenced with the real code (file:line verified).

## 1. Executive summary

The core of the repository is solid and honestly documented: a five-layer separation actually implemented, WENO5-Z transport (order 5) and SSPRK3 correct, geometric multigrid with anti-divergence `solve_robust`, green physical invariants, and a distributed path proven bit-identical np=1/2/4 (multi-patch reflux, MPI coupler, 2x2 de-replication, multi-GPU GH200). What is missing concentrates on three fronts. The central scientific blocker remains entirely open: the conducting boundary is a binary staircase mask (an O(1) error that caps the diocotron rate at +8 percent, flat in resolution), with no cut-cell and no polar grid. High order and AMR are never combined (the production AMR path is hard-coded at order 1 in space plus forward-Euler). Finally, the most sensitive recent fixes are untested (`solve_robust` phase-2, WENO5 in the real 2D flux, invariants in embedded geometry) and two latent robustness bugs exist (`solve_robust` hardens the smoothing irreversibly on the hot path; Euler has no density/pressure floor). Nothing breaks the correctness validated today, but the debt is concentrated and actionable.

## 3. Detailed sections by category

### 3.1 Architecture and design

The five-layer architecture is actually implemented and sound (device-callable `PhysicalModel` concept, `for_each_cell` seam, `EllipticSolver` concept modeled by 3 solvers, AMR roles promoted into types `FluxRegister`/`CoverageMask`/`LevelHierarchy`). The remaining tensions are concentrated on the time/AMR coupling layer.

- **`amr_step_2level_multipatch` public and on the science path, in contradiction with the docs.** Location: `integrator/amr_reflux_mf.hpp:400` (defined in `namespace pops`, outside `detail::` which closes at line 272), called by `examples/diocotron_column_amr.cpp:307-311`, `examples/diocotron_multipatch.cpp:197`, `tests/test_mpi_amr_multipatch.cpp:92`; contradicts `docs/ARCHITECTURE.md:300-322` which states twice that `advance_amr` is the only production entry point and that no `amr_step_*` remains in the public API. The column case (which carries the conducting-boundary science blocker) short-circuits `advance_amr`, `LevelHierarchy` and the whole coupler stack. Recommendation: move `amr_step_2level_multipatch` into `detail::` and rewrite the drivers via `advance_amr` (or a 2-level `AmrCouplerMP`), so that the future cut-cell has a single entry point. Failing that, fix section 8 of ARCHITECTURE.md.

- **`AmrCoupler` (mono-box) duplicates `AmrCouplerMP`, of which it is the strict degenerate case.** Location: `coupling/amr_coupler.hpp:59-133` versus `coupling/amr_coupler_mp.hpp:96-224` (`sync_down`/`compute_aux`/`step`/`update`/`mass`/`max_drift_speed` nearly identical). The multi-patch already reduces bit-for-bit to the mono-box. Recommendation: make `AmrCoupler` a thin-wrapper of `AmrCouplerMP` (`replicated_coarse=true`, regrid disabled) or remove it from the facade; remove the mono-box `coupler_inject_aux` in favor of `coupler_inject_aux_mb`; verify via `test_amr_coupler_mp` (already maxdiff=0).

- **`EllipticProblem` / `make_elliptic_solver` / `FieldPostProcess`: named abstractions but never wired.** Location: `elliptic/elliptic_problem.hpp:46-109`; only callers in `tests/test_elliptic_problem.cpp`. The couplers build `GeometricMG` directly; the `eps` field is inert (the stencil does not read it, the factory throws if `eps` differs from 1). `FieldPostProcess` is only used by `coupler_grad_phi`. Recommendation: choose a trajectory and write it in ARCHITECTURE.md section 7, either wire `make_elliptic_solver` into at least one coupler and make `eps` read by the stencil, or downgrade these types to the explicit status of an unwired design reservation.

- **`subcycle_level_mp`: a 190-line function, 11 parameters, case encoded in a propagated boolean.** Location: `integrator/amr_reflux_mf.hpp:708-894` (flag `coarse_replicated` propagated recursively 849-851). It concentrates `CoarseFineInterface`, `SubcyclingSchedule` and `RegridPolicy` which the docs list as to be promoted. Recommendation: extract the coarse-fine routing (coarse flux sampling, bordering reflux, coverage) into a `CoarseFineInterface` type carrying the replicated/distributed strategy as a policy (type tag) instead of a runtime boolean; the recursion can stay free but no longer see the ownership flag.

- **Wall predicate as `std::function` down into GeometricMG.** Location: `elliptic/geometric_mg.hpp:49,205` (member `std::function<bool(Real,Real)>`), in tension with the low-layer rule ARCHITECTURE.md:18 (no std::function). Bounded (evaluated only at host init, materialized into a `MultiFab` line 65). Recommendation: replace it with a `Predicate` template parameter (inlinable functor), or better, accept directly a masked `MultiFab` filled by the coupler, taking the conductor geometry out of the elliptic layer (5 signatures simplified).

- **grad-phi formula re-inlined in 4-5 places under two non bit-equivalent forms.** Location: `elliptic/elliptic_problem.hpp:105` (multiplicative form `*cx`) versus `coupling/amr_coupler.hpp:102-103`, `coupling/amr_coupler_mp.hpp:156-157`, `analysis/diocotron_invariants.hpp:53-54` (form `/(2*dx)`); the AP scenario `adc_cases/two_fluid_ap/two_fluid_ap.hpp` (`tfap_efield`) reproduces the same `/(2*dx)` form outside the core. ARCHITECTURE.md:278-282 parks the point out of scope (the last IEEE754 bit differs). Recommendation: rewire at minimum the sites without a bit-identical constraint (`diocotron_invariants.hpp`, diagnostic) toward `field_postprocess`; for the couplers, record the link in a comment and group a rewiring during a future numerical re-baseline. Avoid adding a 6th site.

### 3.2 Numerics and science

The numerical core is solid (WENO5-Z order 5 verified in isolation, SSPRK3, MG with `solve_robust`, invariants less than 1 to 5 percent). Three structural gaps limit the science.

- **Staircase conducting boundary: binary 0/1 mask, no cut-cell or polar grid (O(1) blocker toward less than 1 percent). SCIENTIFIC WORK ITEM N.1.** Location: `elliptic/poisson_operator.hpp:16-21,28-41,71-91`, `elliptic/geometric_mg.hpp:62-77,169`. The 5-point Laplacian and the GS smoother always use `diag=2/dx^2+2/dy^2` even when the circle cuts a face at less than dx from the center: Dirichlet phi=0 is imposed on the staircase contour of the centers, not on the physical circle, hence an O(1) error that does not converge under refinement (flat data at +8 percent eff 256/512/1024, `docs/DIOCOTRON_GROWTH_RATE.md:65-105`). No trace of cut-cell, Shortley-Weller or cut fraction in `include/`. Recommendation: implement Shortley-Weller at the embedded boundary (real distance theta*dx to the circle, off-diagonal coefficient `1/(theta*dx^2)` and diagonal modified), via a per-face cut-fraction field computed once from the `active_` predicate, plugged into `gs_color`/`apply_laplacian`/`poisson_residual` (away from the boundary theta=1, bit-identical). Heavier alternative: polar grid. Prerequisite test: `test_poisson_disc` extended measuring the MAX error (not only r less than 0.8R) and the order n=128 to 512, target order 2 up to the circle.

- **High order and AMR never combined: the production AMR path is order 1 in space plus forward-Euler.** Location: `coupling/amr_coupler.hpp:115-118`, `coupling/amr_coupler_mp.hpp:172` (hard-coded `advance_amr<NoSlope, RusanovFlux>` call), `integrator/amr_reflux_mf.hpp:27-39` (a single `mf_advance_faces` per level), versus `examples/diocotron_highorder.cpp:145-156` (WENO5+SSPRK3 only in uniform mono-box, hand-written). Short-term recommendation: expose the Limiter/NumericalFlux of `step` as a template parameter and pass `Weno5` (verify that the levels allocate at least 3 ghosts and that `mf_fill_fine_ghosts_t` provides 3). Medium term: replace the single-stage `mf_advance_faces` of `subcycle_level_mp` with a per-level SSPRK3 with a per-stage Poisson re-solve (the reflux must accumulate the fluxes of the 3 stages). Test: convergence of the AMR diocotron rate versus uniform at equal eff in WENO5.

- **No end-to-end order measurement for WENO5 nor any SSPRK3 test.** Location: `tests/test_weno_convergence.cpp:20-51` (tests only the scalar function `weno5z`), `examples/diocotron_highorder.cpp:145-156` (SSPRK3 loop in the main, untested). Recommendation: extract the SSPRK3 loop into `integrator/ssprk.hpp` (`advance_ssprk3`); add `test_weno_advection_order` (advect a smooth cosine via `assemble_rhs<Weno5>` under SSPRK3, refine n=32 to 128, L1 order greater than or equal to 4.5) and a pure temporal order test (stiff scalar ODE on the imaginary axis, order 3). See also the Tests/Docs section (deduplicated findings).

- **Gradient of phi in centered differences order 2 everywhere: caps the field feedback at order 2 despite WENO5.** Location: `examples/diocotron_highorder.cpp:82-85`, `coupling/coupler.hpp:142-148`, `coupling/amr_coupler.hpp:99-104`, `coupling/spectral_coupler.hpp:84-85`. Recommendation: raise the gradient of phi to order 4 (5-point centered stencil) at least in the high-precision path and in `derive_aux`, to be done AFTER the cut-cell (otherwise masked by the O(1) error). Near the conductor, fall back to order 2 or one-sided. Test: dimensionless ratio gamma over |Re(omega)| before/after.

- **Density/pressure floors absent in Euler; HLLC guards missing.** See the Correctness section (deduplicated finding, severity high).

- **HLL/HLLC unusable for diocotron: `wave_speeds` absent from the Diocotron model.** Location: `operator/numerical_flux.hpp:46-57,62-128`, `model/diocotron.hpp:24-52` (only `max_wave_speed`), `model/euler.hpp:45-51`. Instantiating `HLLFlux`/`HLLCFlux` with `Diocotron` does not compile. Hardly penalizing (divergence-free scalar transport, Rusanov suffices). Recommendation: document that HLL/HLLC are reserved for models with `wave_speeds`, or add a trivial `Diocotron::wave_speeds` (degenerates to upwind, slightly less diffusive than Rusanov, to test before claiming a gain).

- **`solve_robust` hardens the smoothing in a sticky and irreversible way.** See the Correctness section (deduplicated finding, severity high). On the pure numerical side, the overhead is bounded (nu capped at 64) and not observable (no log); add an `fprintf` stderr when phase 2 triggers.

- **WENO5-Z eps=1e-40 subnormal if Real=float (latent, GPU FTZ).** Location: `operator/reconstruction.hpp:55,69-71`, `core/types.hpp:20`. In `double` (current default) 1e-40 is normal. In `float` with GPU flush-to-zero, eps may flush to 0 and give 0/0=NaN on a constant region (beta=0). Recommendation: make eps precision-dependent (`std::numeric_limits<Real>::min()*N`, or a per-type constant); verify the FTZ behavior of the Kokkos kernels.

- **`implicit_solve`: division by the 2x2 determinant without guard.** Location: `model/two_fluid_isothermal.hpp:42-44`. det greater than or equal to 1 in nominal use (no proven bug); purely defensive. Recommendation: `assert(det greater than 0)` in debug, or document the invariant.

- **Strang E/B splitting not quantified in the strongly magnetized regime.** Location: `docs/ALGORITHMS.md:303-322`, `integrator/magnetic_euler_poisson.hpp`. The only test is at E=0 (splitting exact by construction); the diocotron does not use this splitting. Recommendation (low priority): a quantitative test with nonzero E and B (magnetized Langmuir mode) to bound the validity domain of order 2; otherwise implement a combined Boris push.

### 3.3 Correctness and robustness

Clean code, guards already present at the right places (eps in `weno5z`, fmax under the sqrt, k=0 in the FFT, CFL floors). The aliasing in `lincomb`/`saxpy` is harmless (pointwise operations on identical layouts). Two real problems stand out.

- **`solve_robust` mutates `nu1_`/`nu2_` in a sticky and irreversible way on a reused object.** Location: `elliptic/geometric_mg.hpp:126-147` (mutation `nu1_ *= 2`/`nu2_ *= 2` lines 137-138 on MEMBERS line 206), called at each SSPRK3 stage (`examples/diocotron_highorder.cpp:76`, 3 times per step) and at each step (`examples/diocotron_column_amr.cpp:126,164`). If the divergence triggers a single time (unfavorable circle/grid alignment), nu goes for example from 2 to 32 or 64 and stays there for the thousands of following solves: `gs_smooth` does nu sweeps multiplying the smoothing cost by about 16 at each level of each V-cycle, on the hottest path. It is also a side effect hidden behind a method supposed to preserve behavior, which breaks reproducibility (cost dependent on history). Precisely at the high resolutions targeted (HERO_RUN) phase 2 is supposed to trigger. No test covers this path. Recommendation: make the hardening LOCAL to the solve (save nu1_/nu2_ on entry, restore before return, or local variables), so that only the solve that diverges pays the overhead. Add a test calling `solve_robust` twice and verifying that the second restarts at the nominal smoothing.

- **No density/pressure floor in Euler: sqrt(p minus) toward NaN and unguarded HLLC divisions.** Location: `model/euler.hpp:34-41,45-51` (no floor in `pressure`/`sound_speed`), `operator/numerical_flux.hpp:95-127` (HLLC divides by rL, rR, by `(rL*(sL-unL)-rR*(sR-unR))` for sStar, and by `(sL-sStar)/(sR-sStar)`, without guard). On Euler-Poisson/magnetic (Coulomb explosion, Jeans collapse), rho and the internal energy can become very small or negative: `sqrt(gamma*p/rho)` becomes NaN and propagates to the whole field in one step. Recommendation: introduce `rho_min` and `p_min` (members of the model, relative to the reference state), bound before any division; for HLLC guard sStar by an eps at the denominator or fall back to HLL when the denominator is below threshold; add a limit-state test (rho toward 0).

- **`norm_inf` does not do the all_reduce_max under MPI.** Location: `mesh/mf_arith.hpp:38-49` (comment l.48 acknowledges the absence). `GeometricMG::current_residual` explicitly wraps `all_reduce_max(norm_inf(...))` (geometric_mg.hpp:158), so the V-cycle is correct, but any other direct caller gets a LOCAL max: desync of the iteration count between ranks, latent MPI_ERR_TRUNCATE. Recommendation: either do the all_reduce INSIDE `norm_inf`, or rename to `norm_inf_local` plus provide a reducing variant. Verify the other callers before deciding.

- **Division by density without floor in `two_fluid_ap`.** Location: `adc_cases/two_fluid_ap/two_fluid_ap.hpp`, functions `tfap_mstar` and `tfap_div_update_up` (the AP scenario has left the core). In the Hoffart regime (lambda_D toward 0), a cell with n close to 0 produces an infinite velocity then a NaN flux; the centered continuity can make n negative. Recommendation: bound n by `n_min` greater than 0 before each division, or add a positivity limiter after `tfap_div_update`; assert/diagnostic in debug if n less than or equal to 0.

- **`fill_physical_bc`: Dirichlet reflection ng greater than 1 can read beyond the opposite valid cell.** Location: `mesh/physical_bc.hpp:53-95`. With ng=3 (WENO5) and k=3, one reads `domain.lo[0]+2`; if a box at the boundary were narrower than ng, the index would leave the valid zone. Unreachable today (`from_domain` gives much wider boxes). Recommendation: document the assumption (box touching a physical boundary wider than ng in the normal direction) and assert it in debug, or clamp the reflection index.

### 3.4 Performance (run-time)

The run-time of the repository IS the elliptic solver (86 percent of the coupled step, `docs/PERFORMANCE.md`), and the geometric multigrid concentrates the pathologies (small GPU kernels/fences, per-sweep object reconstruction, residual re-evaluated full-level).

- **`fill_boundary` rebuilds a BoxHash (unordered_map) at each call on the hot MG path.** Location: `mesh/fill_boundary.hpp:98` (`const BoxHash hash(ba, suggest_bin(ba))` in `fill_boundary_begin`), whereas `mesh/box_hash.hpp:14-15` advertises a unique amortized construction. Called 2 times per sweep by `gs_rb_sweep` (`operator/poisson_operator.hpp:97,100`): one MG solve does 4 sweeps per V-cycle at the fine level plus nbottom=50 at the coarsest, times several V-cycles, times 2 solves per step, that is thousands of unordered_map constructions per step on a static mesh. Recommendation: cache the BoxHash and the local job list per (BoxArray, ngrow, Periodicity), invalidated only at regrid (stored in the MultiFab or a static cache), or precompute the list of pairs (src, dst, shift, region) and replay it. Net gain serial AND OpenMP. Measure with `bench_amr n=512`.

- **Multigrid: myriad of small kernels plus 2 device_fence per GS red-black sweep.** Location: `operator/poisson_operator.hpp:94-102` (`gs_rb_sweep`: 2 fences plus 2 fill_ghosts plus 2 gs_color), `elliptic/geometric_mg.hpp:180-202`. Root cause quantified in HERO_RUN_AMR.md (GPU slower than 1 CPU core). A V-cycle at the fine level equals 8 fences plus 8 fill_ghosts; the bottom solve equals 100 fences at the coarsest grid where latency dominates. Moreover `gs_color` tests `if (((i+j)&1)!=color) return;` (poisson_operator.hpp:84): 50 percent occupancy, warp divergence. Recommendation, from simplest to most structural: (1) iterate only the cells of the right color (stride-2 indexing) instead of a mask that throws away half the threads; (2) replace GS red-black with a weighted Jacobi or Chebyshev smoother (a single kernel per sweep, minus 50 percent fences); (3) at the bottom solve, switch to a direct solver (tiny grid) instead of 50 GS sweeps. Profile with NSight.

- **`current_residual()` redoes a full-level sweep (Laplacian plus fill_ghosts plus all_reduce) at each V-cycle.** Location: `elliptic/geometric_mg.hpp:89-97,126-147,155-159`. `solve()` calls `current_residual()` before the loop then once per V-cycle; the V-cycle already computes an internal fine residual (`vcycle_rec` line 191): information computed twice per cycle, plus an all_reduce_max (device to host copy) at each cycle. Recommendation: reuse the residual of the first sweep of the V-cycle for the convergence test; compute `norm_inf` only every k cycles if rel_tol is not tight; eliminate the all_reduce_max when `n_ranks()==1`.

- **Serial HOST loops in `compute_aux`/`mass`/`max_drift_speed` of the AMR coupler.** Location: `coupling/amr_coupler_mp.hpp:149-159` (gradient of phi to aux, raw double host loop, no `for_each_cell`), `187-199` (mass), `203-216` (max_drift_speed). Under Kokkos, it reads the unified memory on the host. Recommendation: replace the gradient with `field_postprocess` (already tested, device-callable), the reductions with `for_each_cell_reduce_sum`/`for_each_cell_reduce_max`; verify the bit-identical guarantee (sum reassociated under Kokkos, `for_each.hpp:75-86`) against the mass tests.

- **Temporary MultiFab rebuilt per step/stage/level.** Location: `integrator/amr_reflux_mf.hpp:417` (Uc_old), `419-420`/`462-463`/`736-737` (face flux), `846` (U_old), `elliptic/geometric_mg.hpp:197` (corr per level and per cycle), `integrator/ssprk.hpp:24,28`, `coupling/coupler.hpp:99,104`. On GPU the Arena amortizes, but the deep copy of the coarse remains, and the value-init to 0 of the fluxes is overwritten right after. Recommendation: hoist the persistent MultiFab (face flux, corr per level, R/U1) into reused scratch members, allocate `corr` once per MGLevel; evaluate a partial copy for Uc_old/U_old (only the interface zone). Gain mostly CPU.

- **Berger-Rigoutsos regrid and tagging entirely serial HOST on the hot path.** Location: `coupling/amr_regrid_coupler.hpp:44-81` (`tag_cells`/`grow_tags`/`berger_rigoutsos` plus two host loop nests), with `mf_find_box` O(nboxes) at the heart of a per-cell loop (`integrator/amr_reflux_mf.hpp:579-583`), that is O(ncells times nboxes) at 66 patches. Recommendation: take `mf_find_box` out of the per-cell loop (precompute the covering parent box per patch); port the interp-from-parent and the fine report to `for_each_cell` device; leave the BR clustering on host (cheap on the coarse TagBox) but parallelize the tagging.

- **`mode_amplitude()` recomputes 256 bilinear interpolations 2 to 3 times per step in the hero-run driver.** Location: `examples/diocotron_column_amr.cpp:231-251`, called lines 298 and 301 (identical double computation the same step). `vmax()`/`mass()` are also host loops. Measurement driver, effort S. Recommendation: compute `mode_amplitude()` once per step and reuse; reuse `max_drift_speed` from the coupler for `vmax()`.

## 4. Suggested roadmap

The order accounts for the hard dependencies and brings up first the high-leverage, low-risk work items.

**Phase 0 (fast, no risk, unblocks measurement and docs).** Fix the doc bug recon=3 (`DIOCOTRON_GROWTH_RATE.md:137`) and the README (`:67`). Document `DistributedFFTSolver`. Cache the `fill_boundary` BoxHash (immediate perf gain serial/OpenMP, implicit prerequisite to any later MG profiling). Low cumulative effort.

**Phase 1 (robustness and safety nets, before any large numerical refactor).** Make the `nu` hardening of `solve_robust` local to the solve, then write `test_solve_robust` (the two go together: the test guards the fix and the new local behavior). Add the density/pressure floors in Euler plus the HLLC guards. Fix `norm_inf` (or rename it). Add the missing coverage tests: WENO5 in `assemble_rhs`, recon=1/2 in multipatch AMR, embedded invariants (with the masked overload). Extract SSPRK3 into `ssprk.hpp` and test it. These nets must exist BEFORE the cut-cell work item to measure its gain without silent regression.

**Phase 2 (scientific work item n.1: boundary hugging the circle).** Implement Shortley-Weller (cut-cell) in the Poisson stencil, or switch to a polar grid. Prerequisite: the boundary order test (`test_poisson_disc` extended to the MAX error) must be in place to quantify the passage from order 1 to order 2 up to the circle. This is the only work item that takes the diocotron rate out of its +8 percent cap toward less than 1 percent. Everything else is subordinate to this one for the scientific target.

**Phase 3 (effective high order, after the boundary).** Once the boundary is at order 2, raise the gradient of phi to order 4 (otherwise it becomes the next limiting factor), then combine high order and AMR: first expose Limiter/Flux as a template parameter (immediate order-5 space gain), then per-level SSPRK3 with per-stage Poisson re-solve. Before this phase, settle the architecture debt of the AMR path: move `amr_step_2level_multipatch` into `detail::` and route the drivers via `advance_amr`, so that the per-level SSPRK3 has a single entry point.

**Phase 4 (distributed scaling, ordered by dependencies).** (1) Gather-tags in `comm.hpp` (unblocks dynamic regrid beyond 2 levels). (2) Distributed MG bottom-solve via `DistributedFFTSolver` or agglomeration (unblocks the de-replication of a large coarse; hard prerequisite of hero step 3). (3) Fusion of the MG kernels (red-black without mask, Jacobi/Chebyshev, direct bottom-solve) plus GPU-resident regrid, so that the GPU beats the CPU. (4) Only then, launch the multi-node distributed AMR hero-run and plot the scaling. The AMR variant of the magnetic system M3 comes after, then SAMRAI (M4) last as an external comparison.

**Background refactors (in parallel, opportunistic).** Merge `AmrCoupler` into `AmrCouplerMP`, extract `CoarseFineInterface` from `subcycle_level_mp`, decision on `make_elliptic_solver`/`EllipticProblem`/`FieldPostProcess` (wire or downgrade in the docs), removal of the wall `std::function`, grouped rewiring of the grad-phi formula. To slip in during the natural touches of the concerned files, without blocking the science phases.
